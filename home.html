Cycling in the city

Background

Bicycle-sharing schemes have surged in number since the mid 2000s, with many of the world's largest cities now operating one.

The Citi Bike bike sharing scheme in the United States' largest city, New York City, is still in it’s infancy having started in summer 2013. Given the population of New York and its large number of tourists, the network should be heavily used.

As the bike scheme does not receive any subsidy from New York City, it must maximize its revenue streams to remain profitable and operational, outside of private investment.

Cycling a success?

Aim

The aim of this project is to analyse journeys made on the bike network, since its opening in summer 2013, to assess whether the scheme can be classed as a success. From the analyses, recommendations will be made for maximizing the program’s revenue streams, including marketing and expansion strategies, and a commentary will be provided on the program’s current expansion plans.

This project's recommendations will allow the investors and public to continue to get the most out of cycle power.
